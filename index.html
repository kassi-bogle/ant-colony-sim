Dock Capacity (8 Doors)

Truck unload: 1.5 hrs
12hr shift / 1.5hr = 8 trucks/door (theoretical)
× 75% utilization = 6 TL/door/shift
× 2 shifts = 12 TL/door/day

8 doors × 12 = 96 TL/day
250 TL ÷ 96 TL/day = ~3 days to receive all material

Install Rate

Rack placement: 45 hrs/row × 160 rows = 7,200 hrs
÷ (7 days × 10 useful hrs × 2 shifts) = 52 HC/shift

Cabling (L11 — EOR/TOR only): 5 hrs/row × 160 = 800 hrs
÷ (6 days × 10 hrs × 2 shifts) = 7 HC/shift

What Gates the Timeline

ConstraintDurationBottleneck?
Delivery (8 doors, 96 TL/day)~3 daysNo — finishes by day 3
Install (52 HC/shift, 7,200 labor hrs)7 daysYes — labor is the gating constraint
Cabling (7 HC/shift, L11)6 daysNo — runs in parallel starting day 2
Delivery finishes by day 3. The crew spends the remaining 4 days installing with all material on site. The dock shifts to outbound-only (waste/packaging) for days 4–7.

Key Assumptions

  • Dock cycle: 1.5 hrs/truck (per CB3 throughput model)
  • Shifts: 2×12hr, 75% utilization
  • Rack placement: 45 labor hrs/row (per CB3 model)
  • Rack cabling: ~5 hrs/row — L11 eliminates v7's 27 hrs/row
  • Row constraint: Max 3 workers per row
  • Rack spec: TPU v8 — 28"W × 48"D, L11 (pre-cabled from factory)
  • Truck capacity: ~20 racks/truck (44K lb payload limit)
  • Loading dock: 19,400 sq ft total (~9,700 per door cluster)
  • Staging density: ~400 sq ft per TL staged
  • Excluded: CDU installation, machine population, QA/turn-up
