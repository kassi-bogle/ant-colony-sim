Door Placement — South Wall

Interior Logistics

Delivery path:
Truck → forklift to staging lane → tugger train (4–6 racks) → center aisle → pallet jack into row

Travel: Avg ~200' one-way, <1 min at tugger speed
EquipmentQtyUse
Forklifts4Trailer → staging
Tugger trains8Staging → row (4/DH)
Pallet jacks~34Row placement
8 tuggers × 5 racks × 3 trips/hr = 120 racks/hr
Need: 257 racks/day ÷ 20 hrs = 13 racks/hr

Acceleration Options

Delay admin build-out: Use admin block (~20K sq ft) as staging during sprint. Saves 1–2 days.
South wall knock-out panels: 2–4 removable sections → 10–12 dock positions. Seal post-sprint.
Skip pod partition walls: Wider aisles during install. Build walls after rack placement. Saves ~0.5–1 day.
North wall temp access: Open 2–4 CU bays for direct DH access. Doubles delivery paths. Most aggressive.

Key Assumptions

  • Dock cycle: 1.5 hrs/truck (per CB3 throughput model)
  • Shifts: 2×12hr, 75% utilization
  • Rack placement: 45 labor hrs/row (per CB3 model)
  • Rack cabling: ~5 hrs/row — L11 eliminates v7's 27 hrs/row
  • Row constraint: Max 3 workers per row
  • Rack spec: TPU v8 — 28"W × 48"D, L11 (pre-cabled from factory)
  • Truck capacity: ~20 racks/truck (44K lb payload limit)
  • Excluded: CDU installation, machine population, QA/turn-up
  • Pre-staging: Recommended but not required with 8 doors
