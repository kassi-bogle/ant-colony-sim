OPERATION ANT COLONY — FIELD GUIDE
Applying ant colony stigmergy to TPU rack deployment
🐜 ANT COLONY PRINCIPLES → HUMAN TEAMS
Foragers (Dock Crew) — receive trucks, stage pallets, dispatch carts. One per door bay.
Carriers (Tugger Ops) — transport racks from dock to assigned row. One row per cart = no collision.
Builders (Power Crew) — follow carriers into each row, connect power whips from PDU tap boxes.
Weavers (CDU Crew) — connect liquid cooling manifold after power is confirmed per row.
Loaders (Cable Crew) — in-row fiber/copper, then EOR uplink. Row goes green = online.

Stigmergy: completed rows act as pheromone trails — crews self-direct to next unfilled row without coordination overhead.

🐜 ANT-OPTIMAL SEQUENCE (RECOMMENDED)
Step 1 — Pre-Stage:
All racks staged in Transport Hall before Day 1. Offsite warehouse QA/QC complete. Batch delivery ready to release on schedule.
Step 2 — Delivery Wave:
Start from center corridor outward (rows 6→1, 6→12 simultaneously). Keeps corridor clear. 1 carrier per row = 24 parallel streams per DH.
Step 3 — Follow-On Crews:
Power crew follows delivery by 1 row lag (~4 hr). CDU crew follows power. Cable crew follows CDU. Four-wave peristaltic sweep — M/E/IT never share the same row.
Step 4 — Row Go/No-Go:
Commission per row, not per DH. Enables partial power-up during active delivery. First rows online by hour 18 — 30 hrs before full deployment completes.
